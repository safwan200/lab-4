Introduction

Qiddiya has a Saudi cultural, sports and entertainment project whose idea was announced by the Saudi Crown Prince and Chairman of the Board of Directors of the Public Investment Fund on April 7, 2017 as a qualitative entertainment and social destination, and the foundation stone for the project was laid by the Custodian of the Two Holy Mosques King Salman bin Abdulaziz Al Saud on April 28, 2018 corresponding to Shaban 12, 1439 E. The Qiddiya Investment Company, which is a closed joint stock company launched in May 2018, is specialized in implementing and developing the project's work. The Qiddiya Company has set the implementation plan for the project in a schedule to be implemented over a period of 4 years, ending with the main opening in early 2023, and announced the work of the first phase in July 2019, as it included more than 45 projects and more than 300 activities across the creative, hospitality, entertainment and sports sectors, including the Qiddiya Stadium

city

It is located in the Qiddiya region, southwest of the city of Riyadh, 40 km away from the Saudi capital, and Qiddiya is an hour from King Khalid International Airport, and forty minutes from the center of Riyadh. Al-Qudiyah is a historical area, where the pilgrim road used to pass from Riyadh to Makkah and Madinah before it became modern transportation, and overlooked by the Tuwaiq Mountains, an attractive area uniquely characterized by heritage elements and a distinctive desert view.

naming

The name of the area goes back to the Aba al-Qud road that connected "Al-Yamamah to Al-Hejaz" in the past, and the area included Wadi Al-Qiddiya, which flowed into Wadi Namar and then into Wadi Hanifa.
